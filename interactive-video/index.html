Interactive Video

An interactive video allows the viewer to interact with the content. This is done through the use of hotspots and branching. Hotspots are clickable areas which lead to more information being revealed whilst branching allows the viewer to choose the direction of the narrative.

Some examples on companies that provide this service :

  • Vimeo Interactive Videos
  • Spiel (Company bought by Vimeo)
  • Wirewax
  • h5p
  • Wideo
  • Verse

Characteristics

  • Branching: You can click on the screen to go to different parts of the video
  • Clickable Hotspots: These are basically areas on the video which can be clicked on to reveal more information or to go to a different site.
  • Overlays: This is basically the additional information that appears on the screen once a hotspot is clicked on

Pros

  • Customization touchpoints with branding
  • Recording data of every click
  • They generate greater engagement than traditional linear videos

Cons

  • All of the companies we looked into it offer their services of creation and hosting for a monthly or annual fee, there's no possible to test without signing in.
  • Hosting an interactive video is more difficult and slow than traditional videos. Because of this, there are not many examples on the web, or the examples have been removed
  • The cost of the production of an interactive video is higher without taking in account the development to make it interactive
  • The playback of an interactive video is slower and heavier (not super mobile firendly).
  • In a couple of examples, the video wasn't working on Firefox.
